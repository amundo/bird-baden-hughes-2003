Interlinear Text Research

Last Updated: 20040517

Library  | Stylesheets | Software | Research Publications | Research Support | References

Interlinear text has long been considered a valuable format in the presentation of multilingual data, and a variety of software tools have facilitated the creation and processing of such texts by researchers.  Interlinear text typically consists of a row of source text annotated with one or more rows of morphosyntactic analysis and translation. Vertical alignment represents the correspondence between the elements of each row.  The rows of an interlinear text can represent many different information types: orthographic, phonemic, phonetic and prosodic forms; mophemes and morphosyntactic categories; word-level and phrase-level translations into one or more languages; and any kind of analytical coding or commentary.  The alignment between the various rows may be morph-by-morph, word-by-word, or even phrase-by-phrase.

The aim of this project is to develop open and extensible encoding models for interlinear text, and associated support resources. Here we demonstrate a general-purpose conceptual model of interlinear text consisting of four levels: text, phrase, word and morph, as described in Bow, Hughes, Bird (2003).  The model has been implemented using standard XML technologies, and the purpose of this page is to make available the sources and resources relevant to this model and its implementation.

Software

A tool for editing and manipulating interlinear text was developed based on the 4 level model and available here.

A survey of interlinear text editors is reported in Hughes, Bow, Bird (2004) which provides a comprehensive set of functional requirements for such tools. The following links go to the home pages of each tool studied, while further information on the data not reported is available here.
